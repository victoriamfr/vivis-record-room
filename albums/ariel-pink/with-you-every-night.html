Rating: 4.3 / 5

feels like watching someone try to rebuild a house using the leftover neon rubble from all the other albums. rough around the edges in the way that works just how i like.

Album notes

There’s a strange comfort in hearing him circle back to all his old tricks, but softer. it sounds like a person who’s been through it, still insisting on being ridiculous and romantic anyway. every track grew on me, specially after getting to see the tour for it in Chicago.

I like this most as a “late night, scrolling too much, slightly dissociated” listen.
